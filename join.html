The Osmond lab will be starting January 2021 at the University of Toronto on the St. George downtown campus. If you are interested in working with us, as an undergraduate, grad student, postdoc, or collaborator, please get in touch via email (see Contact page). Please let us know a little about yourself (e.g., your current and past experience with biology and math) and why you would like to join the lab (see Research and Publications pages for some info on what we do). We are very open in terms of topic (within ecology and evolution) but will be best able to help you if what you want to learn/do is mathematical modeling. That said, if your interests are more empirical in nature we could potentially find co-supervisors to help. The Osmond lab values diverse perspectives and backgrounds and strives to provide a welcoming and supportive environment. We look forward to hearing from you!

Prospective undergrads: please get in touch if you are interested in learning about mathematical modelling in ecology and evolution! Possibilities include volunteering, a work-study, a research course, and NSERC research awards.

Prospective grad students: the deadline for applications is in early January (see departmental info). Successful applicants will start the following September. If you are interested in applying please get in touch with me first, ideally an entire year before you want to start as many funding opporuntities (e.g., NSERC PhD fellowships) are due the fall before you start.

Prospective postdocs: there are a number of fellowships available, e.g., the EEB postdoctoral fellowship (typically due in January), NSERC (due in October), and Banting (due in October).
