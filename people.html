Mete Yuksel // PhD Student (joint with the Mideo Group), 09/2022 -

I grew up in Moscow, Idaho, a small town on the rolling hills of the Palouse. I first became interested in theoretical biology as an undergraduate math and statistics student at the University of Idaho. At the U of I, I worked on models of gene drive interventions to suppress vector-borne diseases, Chinook salmon abundance in the Willamette River system, and mechanisms of competitive exclusion in spatially structured populations. I am currently working on the evolution of long-range dispersal and inferring patterns of differential methylation in breast cancer. I am particularly interested in using theory - informed by data and computation - to understand the adaptive consequences of spatial structure and the signatures of space, selection, and species interactions left in descendent genomes. Outside of science, I enjoy podcasts, cooking, spending time outdoors, and exploring the many neighborhoods of Toronto!
